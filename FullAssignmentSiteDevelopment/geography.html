Geography

Airdrie is in a prairie biome, like the majority of Alberta. It has the highest elevation out of any official city in Canada. There is a lot of long, tall grass covering the entirety of the city. Throughout Airdrie there are many paved paths which can be taken for long walks or bike rides.

Most of the houses and buildings were quite recently built, due to the city being made larger at a rapid pace. The city has a rather youthful vibe as a result, which makes it the perfect place to raise a family, with its many parks and playgrounds, social events, and recently built schools, especially in the Rocky View Schools division.

There is a giant water tower located in the centre of town. It was originally built to keep providing people with water for emergency use during the summer when the water pressure was low. However, it hasn't been in use since 1977 due to a reservoir being built, but it still makes for a lovely attraction. It is currently being painted due to the city wanting to preserve it.
